FAQ hairline divider
Why charcoal `#393f46` instead of pure black?
A 26% lightness blue-gray, warmer than pure black, cooler than warm gray. Reads as "industrial primer" or "modernist felt-pen ink" — and the shop is the most-used color on the page (2,649 occurrences).
What's the amber 85%-alpha trick?
The source uses `rgba(216, 158, 46, 0.85)` for the primary CTA fill — softens the saturated mustard into a muted warm gold. Full-pure amber breaks the modernist register.
Why the half-rounded panels?
`4px 4px 0 0` (top) and `0 0 6px 6px` (bottom) signal "tab edge" and "sleeve flap" — a modernist nod to architectural detail without explicit shadow work. 84 + 169 occurrences.
